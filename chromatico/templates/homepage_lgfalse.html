Chromatico

What is Chromatico?

Chromatico is a website that teaches people how to recognize tones and chords by ear using piano and frequency tones based on the Chromatic scale.

How does it work?

When a user signs up, they are then directed to take an assessment testing their tonal recognition skills with frequency-based tones. There are 20 questions and no time limit. Once a user finishes, they are then taken to see their results, where they are assigned a level from 1-3. Level 1 is minimal skills, if any at all. Level 2 has some skill, but they need more training to recognize a wider range of tones. Level 3 recognizes most tones, but not the upper-majority. Level 4 has the ability to recognize tones 100% of the time, and may be able to recognize chords as well. Once a user is assigned their level, they can train through more quizzing. They gain points as they go and once they reach certain quotas, they may take a final exam for their level - which they must score 95% or higher to pass - to level up.

Why it works

Methods used in Chromatico are the same methods used by classical music teachers all over the world, but availabe for free. It encourages users to study hard and learn new concepts. How? Here's one example: The assessment a new user takes not only assigns them to a level, but the assesment also judges what kind of questions a user needs. For example: If a student were to get 6/20 questions correct on the assessment, this would allow the user to choose what type of auditory questions they want- piano-based tones or frequency-based tones. Just because they might not be good at recognizing the frequencies in the quiz, that doesn't mean they can't train with another method. As the user gains confidence in their ability for aural chord recognition and tonal memory, they are then introduced to the new method and trained with it. Chromatico also borrows elements from the Suzuki method- putting more emphasis on learning to hear/play notes than the theory behind them. Once a user reaches level 3, if they desire, they then have the resources to learn about music theory.

Features

Quizzes:

  • Signup quiz with 20 questions and immediate results afterwards
  • Customizable options before training with quizzes(i.e. piano notes, frequencies, etc.)
  • No time limits
  • Positive affirmations after completion
  • Rewards after leveling up

Miscellaneous

  • User signup/login/logout
  • Statistics for each user displayed on their homepage
  • Options for each user, such as changing password, email or deleting account
  • A log of achievements for each user on their homepage
